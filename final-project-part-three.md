# Final thoughts

> When we first discussed project topics, I was inclined to stick to my usual area of interest: immigration policy. However, I decided to step out of my comfort zone to analyze the systems and structures of a sport I grew up watching and playing. Despite being a lifelong fan and player, I had never stopped to look at the internal analytics or the decision-making processes behind the game. This project is not only a reflection of my interests but also of the journey I took; as I created the visualizations, I was shocked to discover how deep the roots of inequity reach. This realization deepened my empathy for the players and lit a fire in me to bring more attention to the league I have spent my life idolizing.
> 
On the design side, I spent a significant amount of time wrestling with my visualizations. I recall spending nearly six hours just making tradeoffs on my viewership graph. I grew to understand that visual instincts are deeply personal and that there is rarely one "right" way to present data. More importantly, when storytelling through data, you must ensure the visuals are both striking and digestible in less than 30 seconds. While I was tempted to use traditional graphs or specific techniques we learned in class, I discovered that the most effective way to tell this story was through relatable, personable visuals, like the basketball court. We all intuitively understand the size of a court, so using it to illustrate the revenue split was shocking to the audience; even without a data background, anyone can grasp the disparity of a whole court versus a tiny fraction of one.
> 
I am incredibly happy with the final visuals and proud of my personal growth throughout the process. The more I "hit my head against the wall" with Tableau, the more intuitively I began to use the software. Learning through trial and error allowed me to understand how different tools function in a way that a standard tutorial never could have taught me.
If I had more time, I would have delved deeper into other aspects of the league, such as revenue infrastructure, media deals, and league expansion. I think many viewers mistakenly assume that Caitlin Clark is the sole reason for these current shifts. However, to truly understand the evolution of the WNBA, you have to look at the historical deals and the work of the Players’ Union, topics I unfortunately did not have the time to cover thoroughly. Nevertheless, I believe this project is a strong starting point for those who may not be familiar with the WNBA’s structure, pay scales, or the broader wage gap.
